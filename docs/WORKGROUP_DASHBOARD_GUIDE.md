# Workgroup Dashboard — Member Guide
## Mid-Mount Ladder Truck Equipment Evaluation

Welcome to the MBFD Workgroup Dashboard. This guide walks you through the dashboard, its pages, and the evaluation process.

---

## Getting Started

1. Go to **www.mbfdhub.com** and click **"Workgroup Dashboard Login"**
2. Log in with your assigned email and password
3. You'll land on the **Dashboard** — your home base for the evaluation process

---

## Dashboard (Home Page)

When you log in, you'll see a summary of your current status:

- **Workgroup** — The workgroup you're assigned to and your role
- **Current Session** — The active evaluation session (e.g., "Day 2")
- **Pending** — How many products you still need to evaluate
- **Assigned Files** — Vendor spec sheets, brochures, and reference documents available for review
- **My Notes** — Your private notes (only you can see these)
- **Shared Files** — Documents uploaded by any team member for the group to review
- **Completed** — How many evaluations you've submitted so far

---

## Pages

### Evaluations

This is where you evaluate products. You'll see a list of all products assigned for the current session. Each product belongs to a category:

- **Forcible Entry Tools** (e.g., 12-inch Cut-Off Saw)
- **Battery-Operated Extrication Tools** (e.g., cutters, spreaders, rams)
- **Vehicle Stabilization** (e.g., stabilization struts)

Click on a product to open the evaluation form.

### Evaluation Form

When you open a product evaluation, you'll rate it using the **SAVER framework**:

| Letter | Category | What You're Rating |
|--------|----------|--------------------|
| **S** | Safety / Capability | How well does it perform its intended function? |
| **A** | Adaptability / Usability | How easy is it to use? Training requirements? Ergonomics? |
| **V** | Value / Affordability | Is it cost-effective for what it does? |
| **E** | Endurance / Maintainability | How durable is it? Are parts available? Easy to service? |
| **R** | Readiness / Deployability | Is it ready for real-world deployment? Reliable? |

Each category is rated **1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent)**.

You can also:
- **Add narrative feedback** — Describe the product's strongest advantages, biggest weaknesses, and any safety concerns
- **Mark deal-breakers** — Flag any serious safety or operational disqualifiers
- **Recommend whether to advance** — Vote Yes, Maybe, or No on whether this product should move forward

**Important**: You can edit and re-submit your evaluation at any time. If you need to change a rating or add feedback after submitting, just go back to the product and update it.

### Files

View all documents related to the evaluation:
- **Assigned Files** — Official documents assigned by the facilitator (vendor specs, data sheets, comparison guides)
- **Shared Uploads** — Documents uploaded by any team member

You can preview PDFs directly in the browser or download any file.

### Shared Uploads

Upload your own documents to share with the workgroup — photos from hands-on testing, additional spec sheets you found, comparison notes, etc.

### Notes

Your private workspace for jotting down thoughts during evaluations. Notes can be shared with specific team members or kept private.

### Results

After evaluations are submitted, the Results page shows:
- **Session progress** — How many evaluations have been completed across the team
- **Category rankings** — Products ranked by score within each category
- **SAVER breakdown** — Detailed scores across all five rating dimensions for each product
- **Top finalists** — Products with the best overall scores, highlighted with gold/silver badges
- **AI Analysis** — An AI-generated summary analyzing the collected evaluation data, uploaded files, and feedback patterns

The Results page updates in real time as team members submit their evaluations.

### Profile

View and update your profile information.

## The Evaluation Process

1. **Review the files** — Start by reading the assigned vendor spec sheets in the Files page
2. **Evaluate each product** — Go to Evaluations, click each product, and fill out the SAVER ratings + narrative feedback
3. **Submit your evaluation** — Click Submit when done. You can always go back and edit.
4. **Check Results** — As the team completes evaluations, the Results page will show the aggregate scores and rankings
5. **The facilitator will review** — Once enough evaluations are in, the committee will use the results to make recommendations to the Health & Safety Committee and Fire Chief Abello

---

## Tips

- **Be thorough** — Your ratings and feedback directly influence which products advance
- **Use the notes page** — Keep track of your thoughts as you review vendor materials
- **Upload useful docs** — If you find additional specs or comparison data, upload it for the team
- **Check back often** — The Results page updates live as teammates submit evaluations
